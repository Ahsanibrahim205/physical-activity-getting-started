Physical Activity
vs. Exercise

  • The terms physical activity and exercise are often used interchangeably to describe the process of being physically active. However, they do mean slightly different things. Physical activity refers to any bodily movement resulting in energy expenditure. Exercise, on the other hand, is a planned and purposeful attempt (at least in part) to improve fitness and health and usually involves structured and repetitive physical movements.
  • There are many benefits in undertaking physical activity, whether that comes in the form of sport or other lifestyle activities such as gardening, brisk walking or simply climbing the stairs.
  • The benefits can be clustered into two broad categories:
    1. Short to medium term – Improvements in day-to-day wellness such as physical fitness, mood state, energy levels and one’s capacity to deal with stress
    2. Long-term – Prevention of chronic (long-term) health problems (such as heart disease, stroke and cancer) and reduction in the risk of premature death. Some of the benefits you possibly would gain are
      • Up to a 35% lower risk of coronary heart disease and stroke
      • Up to a 50% lower risk of type-two diabetes
      • Up to a 50% lower risk of colon cancer
      • Up to a 20% lower risk of breast cancer
      • A 30% lower risk of early death
      • Up to an 83% lower risk of osteoarthritis
      • Up to a 68% lower risk of hip fracture
      • A 30% lower risk of falls (among older adults)
      • Up to a 30% lower risk of depression
      • Up to a 30% lower risk of dementia.
