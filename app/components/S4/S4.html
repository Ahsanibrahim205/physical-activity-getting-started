Achieving your goals for exercise

    There are several factors that can increase the chances of achieving one’s goals for physical activity. The list below can be applied to many other types of goals.

    • Set short-term exercise goals – Reaching short-term exercise goals is a great motivation to continue exercising (i.e. it can boost ‘self-efficacy’). This means starting from where you are now and gradually building up your levels of activity. For example, if someone hasn’t done any exercise for a while, brisk walking for short periods may be better than joining the gym and setting a target to go several times a week. Gradually building up the level of activity, in terms of time, will help to avoid injury and makes increased exercise seem less intrusive. After you have achieved a short term goal set another one, or make a plan with both sort and long-term goals.
    • Write your goals down – Write them down and put them in a place where you can see them everyday!
    • Be creative – Look for creative ways in which you can build exercise programme into your daily routine (such as commuting, e.g. walking or riding a bike instead of using the car). Look for easily accessible forms of exercise and sport activities.
    • Make a list of the benefits of exercise – Knowledge of the health benefits of exercise can help boost motivation to exercise. Write down the ways in which physical exercise will benefit you in particular: including how will you feel when you are implementing your exercise plan? What else will you be able to do as a result of undertaking more exercise?
    • Identify the barriers – Make a list of anything that might get in the way of you achieving your exercise goals and then explore ways you can overcome these potential barriers. For example, if distractions prove to be a problem, have you thought about a training partner? Training with a friend can be effective in terms of the intensity of exercise programme and the commitment you show
